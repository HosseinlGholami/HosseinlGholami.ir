A Deep Dive into the Multifunctional Vehicle Tracker Project at BABT

Role: Embedded Software Engineer (2021–2022) — Embedded R&D, chip/module selection, and firmware integration

Introduction

Between 2021 and 2022, I worked at Basir Andishan Bina Tadbir (BABT) on the development of a Multifunctional Vehicle Tracker. The device was intended to be a cost-effective yet feature-rich telematics solution, providing GSM connectivity, GNSS positioning, and motion-based driver behavior monitoring.

My responsibility was focused on the embedded side: researching GSM/GNSS SoCs, benchmarking commercial modules, and adapting vendor firmware into a working product. This required overcoming limited documentation and a minimal developer community around the chosen platform.

Figure 1: BABT Multifunctional Vehicle Tracker — embedded design centered on MediaTek MT2503.

System Overview

At the heart of the tracker is MediaTek’s MT2503 SoC, chosen for its integration of GSM and GNSS into a single compact and low-power chip. The system interfaces with a 3-axis accelerometer and gyroscope to detect events such as harsh braking or sudden acceleration. The embedded firmware collects GNSS and sensor data, formats it, and sends it over GSM for telematics processing.

R&D and Chip Selection

A key part of my role was benchmarking and evaluating hardware options to balance performance, cost, and available documentation. Several module families were considered:

  • Simcom — SIM868 (with MT2503D), SIM800C32 (with MT6261D)
  • Quectel MC60 — comparable GSM/GNSS modules
  • AiThinker A9 — alternative GSM-only modules

Figure 2: SIM868 module with embedded MT2503D SoC.

After evaluation, MT2503D was selected as the final platform, partly because Simcom and Quectel themselves embed the same MediaTek cores in their commercial modules, confirming the SoC’s reliability for production trackers.

Firmware Integration

To accelerate development, BABT purchased a MediaTek firmware package from new-mobie. My work included adapting and extending this firmware to:

  • Orchestrate the base code, task management, and finite state machine handling
  • Configure GSM services such as SMS, call, GPRS, USSD, and DTMF
  • Enable GNSS data parsing and packet transmission
  • Integrate accelerometer and gyroscope via I²C drivers
  • Apply a Kalman filter to sensor and GNSS data to increase position accuracy
  • Customize build configurations through Makefiles
  • Optimize for low power consumption in continuous vehicle operation

Product Features

Hardware Capabilities

  • Backup battery: 3.7V 450mAh
  • Ability to declare emergency situations
  • Send information via SMS
  • GNSS location accuracy < 10 meters
  • Power cut-off relay for engine immobilization
  • Microphone for listening
  • Operating voltage: 12–36V
  • Low battery warning
  • Speaker connectivity
  • SIM card slot

Software Capabilities

  • Set speed warnings
  • Define geo-fences and trigger alarms
  • Trigger an alarm when the car is started
  • Ability to turn off the car remotely
  • Recharge GPS SIM card via the app

Key Results

  • Validated MT2503 SoC as a stable and cost-efficient platform for vehicle tracking
  • Delivered a working embedded firmware base for commercial tracker products
  • Integrated motion sensors for driver behavior analytics
  • Defined a clear product feature set combining hardware and software strengths

Conclusion

The BABT Vehicle Tracker project highlighted the challenges of working with under-documented SoCs, but also the potential for cost-effective telematics solutions. Through focused R&D, chip benchmarking, firmware integration, and careful feature definition, we delivered a product that combined GSM, GNSS, motion sensing, and a rich set of hardware/software capabilities in a robust embedded package.
